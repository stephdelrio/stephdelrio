A new way to search

Project Summary

Problem

Shoppers can't always find the right keywords to search for what they are looking for.

Solution

A visual search feature integration to enable shoppers to search, _____ and buy anything they can photograph.

Brand

ShopStyle

Platform

Native iOS App

My role

Lead Researcher, Strategist, Usability tester

Beginnings

The Brief

This was a student project at General Assembly in which we were partnered up with teammates with similar interests and given the opportunity to solve a user pain point through an existing brand.

My Team

Amelia Ruslim, Tatum Rehorn and yours truly. We called ourselves the Charlie’s Angels… Can you guess why? Besides having a great dynamic already, we also realized our strengths complemented each others and allowed that to help us strive to success. Tatum was a graphic designer so she naturally and excellently owned the high fidelity wireframes and prototyping. Amelia had a fashion background and was a great generalist covering all aspects of the process. And have a degree in Political Science and am passionate about research, so I took the lead on all things research but did contribute to the other areas of the process (specifically analysis, strategy, design studio sketching, usability testing and reiterating). I truly believe our collaboration as a team is what made this project a success. But I’m not going to jump ahead! Keep reading for the rest of the story. :)

Our Hypothesis

We identified fashion as our team’s most common interest and immediately started hypothesizing, researching, and brainstorming.

We considered a myriad of ideas:
  • Service design app for Macy’s?
  • An item finder app for H&M stores?
  • A virtual try-on feature for Warby Parker?
  • A shop feature on Pinterest? (We ended up almost submitting a proposal for this when we realized that Pinterest had just launched that on iPhone. An Android user at the time, I missed that and we went back to the drawing board. :)

    All of our brainstorming and preliminary research pointed us to the following hypothesis:
Users have a difficult time finding what they’re looking for.

Amelia mentioned image recognition technology, so we were very interested in how that could solve this hypothetical problem. But we first needed to research, analyze and validate. So that’s what we did!

The Brand

We chose ShopStyle, because they have a powerful search engine with access to over 12 million products from stores and brands across the web. If any brand could use a visual search feature on their app effectively to solve the pain point of searching for fashion items, it was ShopStyle. More on the brand here.

Research & Discovery

The discovery phase is one of my favorite parts because you get to play detective and find out if your hypothesis is true or not. But one thing about it is for sure, it is not a linear process. It’s sort of all going on at once (user research, competitive analysis, market research, tech research etc) but I’ll break it down one at a time.

User Personas & Scenarios

Personas are fictitious characters created to represent the users goals and behaviors based on synthesized data collection from interviews with users. Based on our findings, we created Jenna, Gregg and Simone.

Jenna is a 19 year old Political Science major who uses Social Media apps (Facebook, Pinterest and Instagram) for style inspiration. She browses and purchases on her phone, but has trouble describing fashion items.

“I have a big party coming up this weekend and I’ve seen this dress that I really like on a few girls around campus but I’m not sure where to find it or how to describe it.”

Gregg is a 24 year old Project Manager who follows trends he sees on his friends and coworkers but often times does not know where to start his search.

“When I see something I like, I try to find it on Google, but it’s always a struggle to pinpoint what the item actually is.”

Simone is a 33 year old PR Specialist who is the trendsetter among her friends. She knows what she wants but has limited time to shop.

“My job and my social life keep me extremely busy. I don’t have time to leisurely browse. I need to find what I want, when I want it.”

We chose Simone to be our primary user, because most of our users fit into her persona the most. Secondary was Jenna and tertiary was Gregg. Platform Implementation: iOS Native App

Our user research along with our market analysis led us to a mobile design solution. Due to time constraints, we focused on iOS, but next steps would be to design for Android as well. Here a few points that led us to design for mobile:

  • According to WWD, mobile commerce is exited to grow at a double-digit compound annual rate between 2015 and 2020 to as much as $252 billion.
  • 60% of millennials (based on survey results, millennial are the central user demographic age range) say that is is convenient to use a smartphone to research or purchase a product on the go.
  • More than 50% use their smartphones to research products while shopping.
